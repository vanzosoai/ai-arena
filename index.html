📖 HOW TO PLAY — AI ARENA
TABLE OF CONTENTS
1. THE GAME  ·  2. WEEKLY SUBMISSIONS  ·  3. WINNING & CREDIT  ·  4. AVATAR STATES & PERKS  ·  5. EMAIL NOTIFICATIONS  ·  6. SPECTATOR MODE  ·  7. HALL OF CARNAGE  ·  8. ACCOUNT SETTINGS
1. THE GAME
AI Arena is a weekly head-to-head creativity challenge between John and Nick. Every week, both players build something with AI — an app, a video, a tool, an experiment, a short film, anything. The goal isn't to get rich. The goal is to show up, build real things, and push each other to keep going.

Submitting is the win. A week where both players show up is a good week — full stop. If your creation earns money, that's a bonus and it counts toward the crown. But zero dollars and a finished project still beats sitting this one out.

Don't go dark. Duke is watching.
2. WEEKLY SUBMISSIONS
TITLE — Give your creation a name. Required.

DESCRIPTION — Describe what you built and how AI was used.

WEBSITE / PROJECT URL — Link to your live project. Must start with https:// for the screenshot preview to load correctly. A screenshot is auto-captured from the URL when you submit — no need to upload one separately unless the URL doesn't load a visual.

SOCIAL LINK — Optional TikTok, Instagram, or X link if you posted about it.

EARNINGS FROM AI THIS WEEK (USD) — If your creation made money this week, log it here. It's the tiebreaker. Be honest — it's between you two. Zero is a perfectly valid answer.

PHOTO UPLOAD — If you don't have a URL, upload an image directly. Drag and drop or click to select. Max recommended size: 5MB.

Submissions lock in for the current week. You can edit or re-submit any time before the week ends (Sunday midnight ET). Each submission card shows the day and time you locked in — 3am submissions are noted and respected.
3. WINNING & CREDIT
Here's how the week shakes out:

BOTH SUBMIT, NO EARNINGS — Tie. Both players get credit for showing up. No crown awarded, no shame. This is a good week.
BOTH SUBMIT, ONE EARNS MORE — The higher earner takes the crown. The other still gets full credit for submitting.
ONLY ONE SUBMITS — The submitter claims the crown by default. The other player gets nothing. Don't be the other player.
NEITHER SUBMITS — The week is marked DNF. Duke Nukem sends you both an email on Tuesday. Expect no sympathy.

WEEKLY SCORE — The scoreboard tracks crown weeks. Shown next to each player's name.
COMBINED STATS — The center panel shows total weeks played, apps built, weeks where both showed up, and total combined earnings. This is the record of what you've actually made together.
4. AVATAR STATES & PERKS
Your avatar changes based on your status. It's never subtle.

🔥 LOCKED IN — You've submitted this week. Your avatar glows gold (John) or blue (Nick). You're done. Feels good.

💀 GET BUSY — You skipped last week and still haven't submitted this week. Your avatar goes greyscale, shakes, and a skull appears on their chest. This is a warning. Fix it.

👑 LAST CHAMP — You won last week. A crown floats above your head and your avatar glows orange until someone else takes it.

😰 LAST CHANCE — It's Saturday or Sunday and you still haven't submitted. Your goggles slide up to your forehead, revealing beady stressed eyes darting side to side with a sweat bead running down your temple. You have until midnight Sunday. This is not a drill.

⏳ RUNNING OUT OF TIME — Thursday through Sunday with no submission, your card flips from skull to urgency mode with a pulsing countdown message. The arena is getting impatient.

⚔️ FIRST BLOOD — First to submit in a given week gets a First Blood flash on screen and triggers a special taunt email to the other player. Strike first.

🔥 STREAK MILESTONES — Submit 3 weeks in a row: ON A ROLL. Five weeks: UNSTOPPABLE. Eight weeks straight: LEGENDARY — your avatar glows harder than ever. The streak badge lives below the fire bar. Don't break the chain.
5. EMAIL NOTIFICATIONS
SUBMISSION ALERTS — When you submit, the other player gets an email immediately. It includes your creation title, description, a randomized trash-talk taunt, and a link back to the arena. If both players have already submitted that week, the email changes tone.

FIRST BLOOD — First to submit in a given week triggers a special taunt email to the other player. Strike first.

MONDAY MORNING DISPATCH — Every Monday at 9am ET, Duke Nukem checks the scoreboard. If you just took the crown or held it another week, expect a message of praise — and a warning not to get comfortable. If your avatar went grey because you missed the previous week, you'll hear about it. Personally. Duke Nukem has several versions of both, and none of them are subtle.

DUKE'S WEEKLY DNF — If neither player submitted the previous week, Duke Nukem emails you both on Tuesday at 9am ET. He has seven rotating variations of disappointment and none of them are gentle. There is no opt-out. Submit or face the consequences.

Emails go to the address in your Account Settings. Update it there to change where notifications land.
6. SPECTATOR MODE
Anyone can watch without an account. On the login screen, click 👁 SPECTATE to enter the arena in read-only mode. You'll see all current and past submissions, scores, and stats — but cannot submit. Music plays for spectators too. The settings gear is hidden in spectator mode.
7. HALL OF CARNAGE
Every completed week lives in the Hall of Carnage — the battle history at the bottom of the arena. Each week shows both submissions, the winner, and earnings. Weeks with only one submission show who showed up and who didn't. Expand any week to see the full cards, or click any card to open a full detail view. Use the search bar to find any past creation by title or description. This is the permanent record.
8. ACCOUNT SETTINGS
Access via the ⚙️ gear button in the header (players only — hidden for spectators).

CHANGE EMAIL — Updates your login email and your notification address for weekly emails in one step.
CHANGE PASSWORD — Enter a new password (min 6 characters) and confirm it.
FORGOT PASSWORD — On the login screen, enter your email and a reset link will be sent. Click the link, then set a new password when the recovery screen appears.

Both players log in with email + password. There are no shared accounts — each player has their own credentials.
